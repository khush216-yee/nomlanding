App Privacy Policy

Effective Date: April 14, 2026  ·  Last Updated: April 14, 2026

I. Introduction

This privacy policy applies to the nom: swipe, discover, eat application (the "Application") for mobile devices created by Ranveer Ratra & Khush Shah (the "Service Provider") and offered as a free service. The Application is provided on an "AS IS" basis.

By downloading and using the Application, you acknowledge that you have read, understood, and agree to the terms of this privacy policy. This policy explains what information we collect, how we use it, who we share it with, and your rights regarding your data.

II. Information Collection and Use

A. Automatically Collected Information

When you download and use the Application, we automatically collect the following information:

Email address (for account authentication via Apple Sign-In, Google Sign-In, or Cognito email authentication with password and verification code) · Name and display name (collected via Apple Sign-In, Google Sign-In, or provided during registration) · Location data (GPS coordinates for restaurant recommendations and proximity services) · Device information including operating system version, device model, device identifier, and IP address · Crash logs and error data (collected through Sentry error tracking) · Push notification tokens (device tokens sent to AWS SNS for push delivery)

B. User-Provided Information

The Service Provider may require you to provide the following personally identifiable information for an enhanced experience: email address; name and display name; dietary preferences and restrictions; cuisine preferences and dietary tags; home city; phone number (optional); and saved locations and preferences (favorite neighborhoods, search history, dietary filters).

C. Behavioral and Interaction Data

The Application collects detailed information about how you use it, including: swipe behavior and preferences (likes, dislikes, skips — used to build personalized taste profiles); restaurant interactions (saves, cooldowns, view duration, and interaction timestamps); session metadata (occasion, vibe, hunger level, visit feedback); saved restaurants; behavioral events and user interactions (collected through Mixpanel analytics); and search history and browsing patterns.

D. Location Data Usage

We collect your device's precise location (GPS coordinates) to provide location-based services. Location data is used to provide nearby restaurant recommendations and location-based search results. Aggregated and anonymized location data helps us analyze user behavior and improve the Application. Location data is transmitted to Google Places API to enhance restaurant discovery. Google may use this data for their own service improvement per their Maps Platform terms. Users cannot block location sharing with Google Places API without disabling core app functionality.

E. Artificial Intelligence Processing

The Application uses Google's Gemini AI to enhance your experience and provide personalized restaurant recommendations. The AI processes restaurant swipe behavior and preferences, saved restaurants, dietary preferences and restrictions, cuisine preferences, search history and browsing patterns, and aggregated location data. User identifiers are removed before data transmission to Gemini. AI-powered recommendations are a core feature of the Application and cannot be disabled.

F. User Taste Profiles

The Application builds individual taste profiles based on your swipe behavior (likes, dislikes, skips), cuisine preferences, and saved restaurants to deliver personalized restaurant recommendations. These profiles are stored on your device and our servers for as long as you use the Application, used solely for improving your recommendations and our service, not shared with third parties without your explicit consent, and deletable upon account deletion or by requesting data deletion at nomeats.rk@gmail.com.

G. Push Notifications

The Application uses push notifications to deliver time-sensitive information such as recommendations, promotions, and app updates. To enable push notifications, we collect your device's push notification token and send it to Amazon Web Services (AWS) Simple Notification Service (SNS) for delivery. Your device identifier and user account information are associated with this token for targeting purposes. You can manage push notification preferences through your device settings or the Application settings at any time.

H. Analytics and Behavioral Tracking

The Application uses Mixpanel to collect analytics and behavioral data including user identity (email address, name, user identifier), behavioral events (swipes, saves, searches, clicks, session duration), device and session information, cuisine and dietary preferences, and occasion, vibe, and hunger level metadata. Mixpanel acts as a data processor on our behalf, does not sell your personal information, and is not used for targeted advertising. Your data is stored on Google Cloud servers. Mixpanel's processing is governed by Mixpanel's Privacy Policy. You may opt out of analytics collection via the "Share usage analytics" toggle in Application settings.

III. Data Sharing and Third-Party Services

A. Third-Party Service Providers

Authentication Services: Apple Sign-In (Privacy Policy) manages user authentication and email relay services. Google Sign-In (Privacy Policy) manages user authentication and identity verification. Amazon Cognito (Privacy Policy) manages email authentication, password verification, and secure token management.

Analytics and Error Tracking: Sentry (Privacy Policy) collects device OS version, device model, IP address, app version, and crash stack traces. Sentry is configured to NOT capture user session replays, app memory contents, user email addresses, or other sensitive user data. Mixpanel (Privacy Policy) collects user identity, behavioral events, session metadata, and preferences.

Location and Restaurant Data: Google Places API (Privacy Policy) provides restaurant information, photos, reviews, and location data based on your search queries.

Artificial Intelligence: Google Gemini AI (Privacy Policy) processes user behavior patterns and taste profiles (with user identifiers removed) to generate personalized recommendations.

Infrastructure and Notifications: Amazon Web Services SNS (Privacy Policy) stores and delivers push notifications. Amazon Web Services (Privacy Policy) provides cloud infrastructure. AWS does not mine or use your customer data.

B. Data Transmission to Google Services

The Application transmits location data and restaurant queries to Google Places API for search results and restaurant information. It also transmits user behavior patterns and taste profiles (with user identifiers removed) to Google Gemini AI for personalized recommendations. Google's processing is governed by Google's Privacy Policy and standard data retention policies.

C. Authentication and Credential Management

When you sign in with Apple or Google, the Application receives your email address and name from that provider. If you use Apple's email relay feature, Apple will not disclose your actual email address — a relay address will be provided instead. Your authentication credentials are managed entirely by Apple, Google, or Amazon Cognito. The Service Provider does not store or manage your passwords.

D. Data Disclosure

The Service Provider may disclose information as required by law (such as to comply with a subpoena), when necessary to protect our rights or your safety, investigate fraud, or respond to a government request, or with trusted service providers who work on our behalf and have agreed to adhere to this privacy policy.

IV. Your Privacy Rights and Data Management

A. Controlling Your Data

Location Services: You can disable location permissions in your device settings. The Application will not function optimally without location access, and some features will be unavailable.

Push Notifications: You can disable push notifications through your device settings or the Application settings at any time without affecting core app functionality.

Analytics: You can opt out of Mixpanel analytics via the "Share usage analytics" toggle in Application settings.

Crash Reporting: Sentry crash reporting cannot be disabled, as it is essential for app stability, security, and error detection.

B. Account Management

You can create an account by signing in with Apple, signing in with Google, or using Cognito email authentication with a password and verification code. You can delete your account and all associated personal data at any time through the Application settings or by contacting nomeats.rk@gmail.com. When you request account deletion, the process is initiated immediately and all personal data will be securely deleted from our servers within 30 days, except where we are legally required to retain it.

C. Data Retention Policy

User-Provided Data is retained for the duration of your account and for up to 30 days after account deletion. Location and Behavioral Data is retained for 90 days, after which aggregated patterns (without individual identifiers) may be retained indefinitely for analytics. Crash Logs and Error Data are retained for up to 90 days. Push Notification Tokens are retained as long as the device is registered. Mixpanel Behavioral Data is retained according to Mixpanel's standard data retention policies (up to 5 years by default). Users in the European Economic Area and California have the right to request deletion of their data at any time.

D. Your Legal Privacy Rights

Depending on your location, you may have the right to: Access — receive a copy of the personal data we hold about you; Correction — update or correct inaccurate personal data; Deletion — request deletion of your personal data (right to be forgotten); Data Portability — request your data in a structured, commonly used format such as JSON or CSV. To exercise any of these rights, contact nomeats.rk@gmail.com with your request and proof of identity. We will respond to valid requests within 30 days or as otherwise required by law.

E. International Data Transfers

If you are located in the European Economic Area (EEA) or California, your personal data may be transferred to and processed in the United States by Google, Apple, Amazon Web Services, and Mixpanel. This transfer is necessary for the operation of the Application. By using the Application, you consent to this transfer.

V. Security, Children's Privacy, and Updates

A. Data Security

The Service Provider implements encrypted data transmission (HTTPS/TLS), encrypted storage of sensitive user data, access controls and authentication mechanisms, and regular security assessments and updates. However, no security measure is 100% guaranteed. In the event of a data breach, we will notify affected users as required by applicable law.

B. Children's Privacy

The Service Provider does not use the Application to knowingly solicit data from or market to children under the age of 13. The Application does not address anyone under the age of 13. If the Service Provider discovers that a child under 13 has provided personal information, we will immediately delete this information from our servers. If you are a parent or guardian and believe your child has provided us with personal information, please contact nomeats.rk@gmail.com immediately.

C. Changes to This Privacy Policy

This Privacy Policy may be updated from time to time for any reason. The Service Provider will notify you of any changes by updating this page and notifying you via email or in-app notification of material changes. Continued use of the Application is deemed approval of all changes.

D. Your Consent

By using the Application, you are consenting to the processing of your information as set forth in this Privacy Policy now and as amended by us. If you do not agree with our privacy practices, please do not use the Application.

E. Contact Us

If you have any questions regarding privacy while using the Application, or have questions about these practices, please contact the Service Provider via email at nomeats.rk@gmail.com. We will respond to privacy inquiries within 7 business days.
